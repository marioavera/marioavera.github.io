Security and data handling

What the device holds is a draft. The report is your agency’s.

Agencies need a clear answer about where information lives, and the answer starts with what a phone is actually holding. An officer drafts on the device. A supervisor may sign on the officer’s device, or after the draft is transmitted — either way, a signed draft is still a draft. It leaves the device as a PDF, alongside a NIBRS-structured HTML file for your RMS personnel to review. The report exists when your agency processes and files it, in your systems — not on the phone.

On-device

The device holds the officer’s draft.

Draft content, photos, scans, diagrams, and narrative stay on the officer’s device until the officer transmits them. BlueLineNote does not receive or keep a copy.

Separated systems

Licensing does not require content access.

BlueLineNote’s servers validate individual purchases and agency seats. They receive a device identifier and, for metered features, usage counts — not draft content, report content, or attachments.

External systems

No connection to CJIS systems.

BlueLineNote does not connect to NCIC, III, or any CJIS system, and receives no data from them. Every field in the application starts blank; all information in a draft is entered by the officer. The device remains subject to your agency’s mobile device management, encryption, and access-control policy, as it already is for every application on it.

Field use

Core documentation works without dependable signal.

Officers can continue capturing field information in areas with weak or unavailable connectivity.

Agency policy

Retention remains an agency responsibility.

Discovery, retention, records handling, and device-management obligations remain subject to applicable law and agency policy.

National reporting standards

Structured around the information agencies already need.

BlueLineNote includes workflows informed by NIBRS and MMUCC concepts to help officers organize report information more consistently. Final validation, acceptance, and submission remain subject to each state, agency, and RMS environment.

A defined role

A mobile reporting system

The DOJ Bureau of Justice Assistance Standard Functional Specifications for Law Enforcement Records Management Systems, developed by LEITSC, lists a mobile reporting system as a standard internal data exchange for incident reporting and traffic accident reporting. That is the role BlueLineNote occupies. There is no federal certification program for this category, and the specification describes a role rather than endorsing any product.

Your RMS stays the record

In front of the RMS, not in place of it

BlueLineNote produces a NIBRS-structured HTML file alongside the PDF. Your RMS personnel review it and, subject to your RMS’s link capabilities, may use it for injection into your system of record. BlueLineNote does not submit to NIBRS and does not write to your RMS.

Agency portal

Your department controls its own seats.

Licensed agencies get a private portal. Your authorized point of contact signs in and manages the agency’s roster directly — assign a seat the day a new officer needs it, reclaim one the day someone transfers out. No support ticket. No waiting on a vendor. No phone tag.

Self-service

Assign a seat when the officer needs it.

Your point of contact assigns an open seat and passes the officer their activation key. New hire on Monday, working on Monday — your roster does not sit in someone else’s queue.

Turnover

Reclaim a seat the day someone leaves.

Transfers, retirements, separations. Cancel the seat and it returns to your available pool. The seat is re-keyed automatically, so the departing officer’s key stops working.

Accountability

Every action is on the record.

Who asked, what they asked for, when, and what happened. Your agency sees its own full activity trail. Nothing changes without a record of who requested it.

Controlled access

Only the people your agency names.

Portal accounts are created only by BlueLineNote, only for contacts your agency authorizes, and only on a work email address. Up to two contacts, so coverage does not depend on one person.

Growth

Request seats or branding without a meeting.

Need seats next quarter? Updating your letterhead? Submit it from the portal with your artwork attached, and the request is on file with the date and the person who made it.

Boundaries

Seat administration only — never report content.

The portal manages licensing. It has no path to officer drafts, reports, photos, or narratives. That content stays on the officer’s device until transmitted to your agency, where neither the portal nor BlueLineNote can reach it.

Requests submitted through the portal are reviewed and applied by BlueLineNote, then reflected back to your agency — so your roster stays accurate and every change stays documented.

Frequently asked questions

What agencies usually ask first.

How does the 30-day agency pilot work?

Your agency runs the full product — complete Pro features, your branding on exported PDFs, and your own management portal — for 30 days, extendable to 45, with no commitment. If your agency decides to continue, the pilot converts to a subscription and your contract term begins after the pilot ends, so none of your paid time is spent evaluating. If you decide not to continue, you owe nothing. To start one, email sales@bluelinenote.app.

Does BlueLineNote work without reliable signal?

Core field documentation is designed to work offline. Functions that depend on external services or email availability may require connectivity.

What is actually stored on the officer’s device?

The officer’s draft. An officer drafts on the device; a supervisor may sign on that device or after the draft is transmitted, but a signed draft is still a draft. It leaves the device as a PDF, with a NIBRS-structured HTML file for your RMS personnel to review. The report exists once your agency processes and files it — in your systems, not on the phone. BlueLineNote stores no server-side copy of either.

Does agency licensing allow BlueLineNote to read officer drafts or reports?

No. Licensing validates access and seat status. BlueLineNote’s servers receive a device identifier and, for metered features, usage counts — never the content an officer enters.

Who manages our seats once we are licensed?

Your agency does. Licensed agencies get a private portal where your authorized point of contact can view every seat and activation key, assign an open seat to an officer, reclaim a seat when someone leaves, and request additional seats. Canceled seats are re-keyed automatically, so the old key stops working. Every action is recorded and visible to your agency.

Can the agency portal see our officers’ reports?

No. The portal administers licensing and seats only. It has no access to draft or report content, photos, or narratives — that content stays on the officer’s device until it is transmitted to your agency.

Is BlueLineNote a replacement for an RMS?

No. BlueLineNote is a mobile reporting system that sits in front of your RMS. It can capture officer and supervisor sign-off on the device and carry that approval block into the exported document, but a signed draft is still a draft — the RMS remains the system of record, and the report exists once your agency files it. Retention, validation, and submission continue through your established process and systems.

How does BlueLineNote relate to CJIS?

BlueLineNote does not connect to NCIC, III, or any CJIS system and receives no data from them. Every field starts blank and is populated by the officer. The CJIS Security Policy applies to entities that access, process, store, or transmit Criminal Justice Information; BlueLineNote’s servers receive no draft or report content. The device itself stays under your agency’s existing mobile device management, encryption, and access-control policy. The FBI CJIS Security Policy, version 6.0 and later, is publicly available and may be shared without restriction — we are glad to work through it with your LASO or your state CJIS Systems Officer, who are the authorities on how it applies in your state.

Does BlueLineNote guarantee NIBRS or MMUCC acceptance?

No software can universally guarantee acceptance across every jurisdiction and RMS. BlueLineNote provides structured workflows informed by national standards, while final requirements remain controlled by the applicable state, agency, and system.

Can an agency test BlueLineNote with a small group?

Yes. Agencies can discuss a limited evaluation, deployment approach, seat count, branding, and workflow configuration through the agency contact form.

Privacy

Your reports stay on your device.

Last updated: June 2026

Your reports stay on your device

All reports, notes, scans, and other content you enter into BlueLineNote remain on your device and are never transmitted to the developer or any external server. The developer does not have access to your notes, reports, scans, or any content you create in the app. BlueLineNote does not provide cloud storage or remote databases for your content.

Backup and restore

You can create a password-protected backup of your saved records and scans. The backup is a single encrypted file, locked with a password that only you know — the developer cannot open it. You choose where to save it (your own Files, your own cloud storage, or email) using your device’s share sheet, and you restore by selecting that file. Backup files are never sent to or stored by the developer.

Licensing and feature limits

To validate Pro purchases, activate agency seats, and enforce free-tier usage limits, the app sends a device identifier — and, for metered features, a count of how many times those features have been used — to the developer’s server (api.bluelinenote.app).

This information is used only to operate the free, Pro, and agency licensing. It contains no personal information and none of your report content, and it is never sold or shared with advertisers or other third parties for advertising or tracking.

Agency portal

Licensed agencies may be given access to an agency portal for seat administration. The portal handles licensing information only — seats, activation keys, and the requests an agency’s authorized contact submits. It contains no report content and provides no access to notes, reports, scans, or narratives, which remain on each officer’s device. Portal accounts are created only by BlueLineNote for contacts the agency authorizes.

Anonymous usage analytics

The app collects a small amount of optional anonymous usage data — for example, which features are opened, how long the app is used, your subscription type, device model, operating system version, and app version. This data is not linked to your identity, contains no personal information and none of your report content, and is identified only by a random value that resets if the app is reinstalled. It is not sold or shared with advertisers. You can turn it off at any time under Backup & Restore → Anonymous Usage.

Legal requests

We may disclose the limited information we hold if required by law or in response to a valid legal request. That information is limited to a randomly generated device identifier and licensing or usage records — it is not your name, account, or any personal identifier. Your reports, notes, and scans remain on your device, are never transmitted to us, and are not accessible to us — we cannot produce that content.

Data retention

We keep the limited licensing and anonymous usage data described above only as long as needed to operate the free, Pro, and agency licensing and to keep the service working. Because this data is tied only to a random identifier and contains no personal information, it is not linked to you. You can stop analytics collection at any time under Backup & Restore → Anonymous Usage.

Security

We use reasonable measures to protect the limited data we collect in transit to and on our server. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, but because your reports, notes, and scans never leave your device, the most sensitive information you create is never exposed to us or to anyone in transit.

Platform services

The app uses platform services such as in-app purchases and speech recognition. Depending on your device, these are handled by Apple or Google under their own privacy policies.

Your responsibility

You are responsible for securing your device and for how you store, manage, and use any information you enter into the app. You are also responsible for following your department’s or company’s policies, procedures, and applicable laws governing the documentation, handling, retention, and sharing of any information you record using BlueLineNote.
